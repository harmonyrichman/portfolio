My Journey

June 2018

Organizer, Speaker

ReImagining Employment Summit

I partnered with Cornell University, the Brooklyn Chamber of Commerce, WeWork, and Defy Ventures to create an employment summit that addressed fair and equitable hiring practices in NYC.
The event featured a panel of Executive Directors, Chief Human Resource Officers, and employers who hire justice-involved individuals as part of their business model to educate attending businesses on the business, tax, and societal benefits to practicing equitable hiring.
I led a hiring training for over 55 public and private companies to familiarize businesses with the proper procedure regarding the Fair Chance Act and Article 23-A.

April 2018

Founder

GOSOArts

While working as Employment Development Manager at Getting Out & Staying Out (GOSO), I founded GOSOArts, a comprehensive art program that met on a weekly basis.
GOSOArts allowed GOSO participants to work with different organizations that focused on a wide variety of subjects in the arts including music, dance, spoken word, improv, and theater.
The first year, GOSOArts hosted GOSO's first Hip Hop Competition that hosted over 150 attendees, featured celebrities and artists, and allowed participants to perform their original pieces for the Harlem Community.
The second year culminated in a showcase, Roses Out Of Concrete, that was featured at The Apollo Theatre in Harlem. Roses Out Of Concrete was performed as a series of vignettes written by GOSO participants that outlined the trauma and resiliency experienced by communities entrenched in America's criminal justice system.
